Abstract


Numerous Diffusion Models(DMs) distillation methods have recently been proposed to reduce the computational overhead associated with multi-step inference. However, these methods either suffer from performance degradation when strictly simulating the original ODE trajectory or produce undesirable results in alternative output domains when attempting to reformulate a more efficient trajectory. In this work, we synergistically combine trajectory simulation and reformulation methods to achieve Hyper-SD, leveraging their complementary strengths. Firstly, we Accelerated DMs by introducing a timesteps-segment method to progressively perform consistency distillation, which more precisely maintains the original ODE trajectory by obtaining an intermediate state between the score-based model and the consistency distilled model. Secondly, we Boosted the distilled model by incorporating human feedback learning (RLHF) to compensate for the loss during acceleration and even surpass the performance of the original DMs. Thirdly, we Completed our model by incorporating the score-distillation to achieve the ideal all-timesteps consistency. Lastly, we Flexibled our methods by training Low-Rank Adaptor(LoRA) in all experiments. Extensive experiments and user studies demonstrated that Hyper-SD achieved the new State-of-the-Art for both SDXL and SD15 from 1 to 8 steps inference.

Pipeline


HyperSD take the two-stage Progressive Consistency Distillation. The first stage involves consistency distillation in two separate time segments: [0, T/2] and [ T/2 , T] to obtain the two seg- ments consistency ODE. Then, this ODE trajectory is adopted to train a global consistency model in the subsequent stage
